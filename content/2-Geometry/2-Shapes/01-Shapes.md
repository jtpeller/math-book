## Overview

A {{< dfn "Shape" >}} is what is formed when three or more lines intersect and form a geometric object with area and a perimeter. Put simply, a shape is a geometric object that has an area.

### Area and Perimeter

{{< dfn "Area" >}} describes the space that is "closed off" by the lines. Think of it as all the points that a particular shape contains.

{{< dfn "Perimeter" >}} describes the lengths of all lines that compose the shape.

### Composition

Shapes are composed of *edges* which meet at *vertices*.

An {{< dfn "edge" >}} is the line that forms one side of a shape.

A {{< dfn "vertex" >}} describes the point at which two lines (edges) join. As a consequence of this, an angle is formed by the two edges at this vertex.

## Types of Shapes

An {{< dfn "Irregular Shape" >}} is one where its sides and interior angles are not all the same.

An {{< dfn "Regular Shape" >}} is one where its sides and interior angles are all the same.

A {{< dfn "Cyclic Shape" >}} is one whose vertices all lie on a single circle. In other words, this shape is {{< dfn "inscribed" >}}.

A {{< dfn "convex" >}} shape is one such that all of its interior angles are less than $180^\circ$.

A {{< dfn "concave" >}} shape is one such that at least one of its interior angles are greater than $180^\circ$.

An {{< dfn "equilateral" >}} shape is a shape such that all sides are the same length. For instance, a square is equilateral.

A {{< dfn "regular convex" >}} shape is a regular shape that is also convex.

A {{< dfn "simple" >}} shape is one that does not intersect itself.

A {{< dfn "equiangular" >}} shape is one such that all corner angles are equal.

A {{< dfn "tangential" >}} shape is one such that all sides are tangent to a circle. In other words, the circle is inscribed inside this shape.

## Properties of Shapes

{{< dfn "Congruence" >}} describes a property of two shapes such that they have the same shape and size.

- Two line segments are congruent if their lengths are equal.
- Two angles are congruent if they have the same measure.
- Two circles are congruent if they have the same diameter.
- To quickly make a congruent shape, you can just move the shape to a new location (like a copy-paste).

{{< dfn "Similarity" >}} describes the relationship between two shapes such that they have the same "shape". In other words, the shapes are just about the same, but they have been moved, rotated, reflected, or scaled:

- {{< dfn "Translation" >}} is an action done to a geometric object where the shape is moved across the plane.
- {{< dfn "Rotation" >}} is an action done to a geometric object where the shape is moved around a fixed point on a plane.
- {{< dfn "Reflection" >}} is an action done to a geometric object where the shape is mirrored (aka, flipped) across a line.
- {{< dfn "Scaling" >}} is an action done to a geometric object where the shape is shrunk or enlarged. That is, the area the shape contains shrinks or grows, but the overall shape remains the same.
