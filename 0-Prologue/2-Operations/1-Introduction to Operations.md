## Overview

Operations are essentially means of manipulating numbers in some specific way. There are plenty of operations across all of mathematics, but this section will introduce the basic Arithmetic Operations: add, subtract, multiply, and divide.

## Addition

Addition is the act of aggregating two numbers together.

> Addition answers: "What happens when I combine one group of things with another group of things to make a larger group of things?"

For instance, if I have 5 apples in one basket, and 10 apples in another basket, and combine them into a bigger basket, how many apples would I have?

Well, the first step is to realize that the final basket would have the 5 apples from the first basket, and 10 apples from the second. To represent this, you would _add_ the two quantities together. That would result in 15 total apples!

The addition operation is represented by the $+$ sign. To represent the situation above, you would write:

$$5 + 10$$

Which represents the actual act of adding the two numbers. This is known as an **_expression_**, and can be thought of as a "mathematical phrase".

The next step when writing an expression is to simplify it. That is, make the expression as consolidated as possible. In this case, you would add the two numbers:

$$5 + 10 = 15$$

As it is written above is an equation. An **_equation_** defines an equality between the two sides of the equation. Each side of the equation are expressions, and each side of the equation MUST be equivalent (that is, represent the same quantity).

**_Equal signs_** (that is, the $=$) are symbols used to represent whether two expressions are equal. That is, the left side represents the _exact same quantity_ as the left side.
